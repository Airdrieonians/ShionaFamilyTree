Dryden Liddle was born on 5 May 1914 in Loanhead. He had a non-identical twin brother - Archie and 5 other brothers and sisters. As a young man he worked as an marine engineer at McTaggart Scott in Loanhead. He married Jemima (Mamie Ashcroft Grant) from Bonnyrigg, in 1937 when he was 23. At the time he lived at 20 Kennington Avenue in Loanhead. In 1940 he lived at 16 Station Road Loanhead with his wife Mamie. By 1945 he had moved away from Loanhead and was living at 254 Colinton Mains Road, Edinburgh, Midlothian. His son Dryden Ashcroft Liddle was 5 years old in 1945. His older brother George also lived on this road. Dryden and family moved to Greenock to work in the ship yards and then he moved to Sunderland County Durham to work there for a while. His daughter Shiona Grant Elaine Liddle was born there in 1959. He moved back up to Edinburgh to 6 Camus Road West, Fairmilehead in the early 1960's when Shiona was around 3 years old. Then to 11 Middleshade Road, St Andrews Fife in the mid-sixties to work in Auchtermuchty at John White and Sons, a foundry and engineering works, as a casting engineer. In the early 1970s he moved to 2 Newmills Road, Balerno, Edinburgh, Midlothian. He started a company: Bridgeforth Engineering Just across the Forth Bridge in Inverkeithing. He died of a heart attack while on holiday with his wife Mamie on 28 January 1981 in Malta at the age of 66. He had type 2 diabetes and had several other heart attacks previously.
