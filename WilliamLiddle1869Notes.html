William Liddle was born on 11 June 1869 in the mining village of Whitehill, 
Dalkeith to William Liddle and Agnes Archibald. 
He would go on to call a daughter Agnes Archibald Liddle, as would his 
brother George. 
At the time his father (a coal miner) was 32 and his mother was 34. 
He married Jane Dryden on 27 March 1891. His brother George and then his 
other brother Alexander went on to marry her sister Georgina Dryden. 
They had seven children in 21 years - with two sets of twins. 
He died on 27 November 1936 at 23 Mayburn Terrace Loanhead, Midlothian 
at the age of 67 of chronic bronchitis and asthma. 
In the 1891 Census and at his marriage (also in 1891) William is a Coal Miner. 
In the 1901 Census he is a Restaurateur employing people. 
In the 1911 Census he is back to being a Coal Miner. 
Then he is described as a 'Confectioner' on his son George Alexander Liddle's 
death certificate (died 1965). .
